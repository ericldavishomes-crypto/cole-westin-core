


The smell of roasted coffee drifted upstairs, but Eric and Cole were already focused on the grind. "Iron sharpens iron, Champ," Eric said, taping his wrists.

It was 6 AM, and the gym was their battlefield. Their rivalry was fierce—a constant, unspoken duel—but their respect was absolute, a brotherhood built on shared strength and relentless dedication.

Eric stepped up to the pull-up bar first. He knew Cole's larger, more muscular frame had the advantage, but he didn't care. He pulled with controlled power.

One. Two. Three...

By ten, his lats were burning. At fifteen, his wide solid shoulders and back muscles began to tremble, his grip tightening. He fought for sixteen, exhaling sharply, his back veins popping under the strain. Seventeen was a pure battle of will; his chin barely cleared the bar, face tight with agony, but he prevailed.

He dropped down, chest pumped and heaving with every breath, proud in the sheer effort he'd just displayed.

Cole smirked, stepping up immediately.

He didn't even warm up. With a heavy exhale, he cranked out pull-ups with perfect form—a slow, punishing tempo.

Twenty-five... Twenty-six...

He was a machine, his muscles working in unison. By twenty-nine, the struggle was real, his breath hitting a snag, his body demanding he quit.

Eric pressed his hand firm on Cole's lower back.

Cole felt it there, locked his gaze forward and hammered out number thirty.

As Cole dropped, Eric punched him in the shoulder, a huge grin on his face. "Unreal, Champ. Thirty clean?

That's my boy, always next-level work," Eric said, his voice filled with genuine admiration. "I told you, you're becoming a beast.

Keep that pace up, and I'm gonna have to step up my game even more, asshole."

Cole nodded, sweating but smiling. "Thanks, big bro. But I was just trying to keep up with that seventeen you put down."

They both knew the score, and they both knew the lesson:

Iron sharpens iron. Always.
