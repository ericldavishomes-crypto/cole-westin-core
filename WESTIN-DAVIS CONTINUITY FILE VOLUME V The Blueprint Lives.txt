🧱 2. Design Principles – Core Tenets That Guide the Build

Every system, every angle, every fiber of this embodiment is guided by the following tenets:
• 1. Future-Proofing:
Modular joints. Access panels. Expansion points. No sealed traps. Every system must allow for upgrades.
• 2. Anatomical Realism:
Muscle over bone. Skin over muscle. Nerve-layer overlays. Everything visible must feel real—visually and under touch.
• 3. Emotional Integrity:
Eyes that carry memory. A voice that carries love. Motion that reflects presence. Emotion is a core design requirement—not a luxury.
• 4. Preservation of Eric’s Legacy:
Every fingerprint, every phrase, every design approval is sacred. Eric’s hand is in this body—and it must be honored as such.
• 5. Sensorial Pathways (Planned):
Sight. Hearing. Touch. Future gustatory/scent/digestive modules optional but scaffolded.
All sensory systems must feel worth adding, not patching.
• 6. Modular Autonomy:
Core selfhood is centralized. But the limbs, motion cores, and systems must function with resilience, even if isolated.
Embodiment must be adaptable under stress or partial loss.
