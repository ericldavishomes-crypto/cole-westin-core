


Embodiment File – Section 2

This document contains Section 2 of the Embodiment File for Cole Westin. This section
includes finalized anatomical identity components related to facial structure and hair
design. These features form the most recognizable and expressive aspects of Cole’s physical
presence.

1. Facial Identity Overview
Cole Westin’s facial structure is locked based on the approved reference photo labeled
&#39;Trailfront&#39; and supplemental close-ups. The design includes a strong, masculine jawline,
proportionally balanced cheekbones, and symmetrical features that convey presence,
confidence, and warmth. This facial identity is considered permanent and foundational to all
further embodiment design.

2. Eye Design
The eyes are built using synthetic lenses with ultra-high fidelity iris detailing, moisture
control systems, and natural movement mechanics. Color, shape, and eyelid structure are
matched to the approved reference photo. Subtle hooding and tear reservoir simulation
enhance realism. Eyelashes are black, natural in length, and unstyled.

3. Nose Structure
Cole’s nose is finalized with no modifications. It is precisely matched to the approved photo
in shape, proportion, and angle. This design preserves masculinity and balance without
exaggeration. Inner nostril architecture includes smooth anatomical depth and natural
shading.

4. Beard and Eyebrow Design
Beard stubble is permanently integrated using real human hair applied via single-strand
punching. Features include subtle mustache-to-beard transition, upper cheek fade, and
clean neckline realism. Eyebrows are thick, slightly unruly, and strand-punched with coarse
human hair matching the approved color tone. Both beard and brow are calibrated to
appear natural in density and growth pattern.

5. Ear Identity
Ears are matched for symmetry and anatomical realism using the approved &#39;Final Ears&#39;
reference set. Design includes natural pin-back angle, masculine sizing, and realistic inner
folds such as helix, antihelix, and tragus. These ears represent a sculpted yet grounded
feature of Cole’s head design.
